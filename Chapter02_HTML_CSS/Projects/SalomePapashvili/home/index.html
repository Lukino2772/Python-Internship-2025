Mercury

The smallest and closest planet to the Sun. A world of extremes, Mercury endures scorching days and freezing nights. No atmosphere, no nonsense — just rock and heat.

Venus

The hottest planet in the solar system. Cloaked in toxic clouds and volcanic plains, Venus is a blazing inferno with hurricane-force winds. Beautiful but deadly. Earth

Our home. The only planet known to harbor life, Earth is a delicate blue oasis wrapped in a breathable atmosphere, teeming with oceans, forests, and endless diversity.

Mars

The red planet. Once possibly home to rivers and oceans, now a dry, dusty world with giant volcanoes and deep canyons. Humanity’s top contender for future colonization.

Jupiter

The king of planets. A colossal gas giant with raging storms—most famously the Great Red Spot—and a retinue of fascinating moons like Europa and Ganymede.

Saturn

Adorned with breathtaking rings of ice and rock, Saturn is a gas giant of elegance and mystery. Its moons, including Titan, hint at otherworldly possibilities.

Uranus

An icy, tilted giant that spins on its side. Uranus has a bluish hue due to methane gas and is known for its faint rings and cold, distant atmosphere.

Neptune

The farthest known planet. Deep blue and turbulent, Neptune is a windy ice giant with supersonic storms and the distant moon Triton orbiting in reverse.
